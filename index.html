How OTA Commission Impacts Net ADR

Most independent hotels evaluate performance based on gross ADR. However, net ADR often tells a different story.

A room sold at ₹6,000 with a 20% OTA commission results in a net realized rate of ₹4,800. Over time, this commission erosion significantly affects retained profitability.

For mid-size hotels operating with 60–70% OTA dependency, monthly commission impact can reach several lakhs. This directly reduces gross operating profit without increasing occupancy.

Optimizing channel mix does not mean eliminating OTAs. It means strategically strengthening direct booking share while maintaining visibility.

Even a 10% shift toward direct bookings can materially improve net revenue retention.

RevPAR vs Occupancy: What Owners Often Misunderstand

Occupancy alone is not a reliable performance indicator.

A hotel operating at 75% occupancy with aggressive discounting may generate lower overall profitability than a hotel at 60% occupancy with optimized ADR.

RevPAR (Revenue Per Available Room) combines both occupancy and pricing discipline.

RevPAR = ADR × Occupancy.

Strategic pricing refinement, especially during high-demand cycles, can increase RevPAR without requiring additional room inventory.

Revenue optimization focuses on balancing rate integrity with demand elasticity — not simply filling rooms.

Dynamic Pricing Is a Margin Strategy, Not a Marketing Tactic

Many independent hotels maintain static pricing across seasons, relying on historical assumptions rather than demand signals.

Dynamic pricing adjusts rates based on occupancy pace, local events, demand trends, and booking window behavior.

This is not a marketing tactic — it is a margin discipline.

Demand-based pricing allows hotels to:

  • Protect ADR during peak demand
  • Stimulate occupancy during low season
  • Improve RevPAR stability
  • Reduce unnecessary discounting

Over time, structured pricing discipline produces more predictable retained profitability.
